Michael Jordan

To the right Michael Jordan and to his left his Hall Of Fame teammate Scottie Pippen.
Here is a timeline of a few of his career achievements:
1981: McDonald’s All-American and accepts a scholarship to University of North Carolina.
1982: Makes game-winning shot in NCAA tournament final against Georgetown.
1984: Selected No. 3 by the Chicago Blils in the NBA draft
1985: Selected rookie of the year after averaging 28.2 points, 6.5 rebounds and 5.9 assists.
1988: First MVP
1993: Jordan is Finals MVP for the third time in a row.
1993: Jordan announces his retirement.
1995: Jordan rejoins the Bulls with a two-word fax: “I’m back.”
1998: Jordan scores 45, including a title-winning jumper. Jordan collects a sixth Finals MVP.
2009: Jordan is inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame
